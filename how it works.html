What makes a machine intelligent?

  1. Thinking humanly
  2. Thinking rationally
  3. Acting humanly
  4. Acting rationally


The first two ideas concern thought processes and reasoning, while the others deal with behavior. Patrick Winston, the Ford professor of artificial intelligence and computer science at MIT, defines AI as "algorithms enabled by constraints, exposed by representations that support models targeted at loops that tie thinking, perception and action together."
